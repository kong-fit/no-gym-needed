Hotel Room
Reset

Built for the gap between checking in and your first meeting. No mat, no noise, nothing that requires you to know what's underneath the carpet.

The circuit

All standing — no mat required. Do both rounds back to back.

01

Bodyweight Squat

Feet shoulder-width, sit back like you're reaching for a low chair. Wakes up your legs faster than anything else on this list.

Weight in your heels, chest stays tall.

15 reps
—
02

Incline Push-Up (off the desk or dresser)

Hands on a sturdy piece of furniture, body in a straight line. Easier on the shoulders than floor push-ups after a long flight.

Pick furniture that won't slide — test it before you start.

12 reps
—
03

Standing Marches

Drive your knee up to hip height, alternating sides at a steady pace. Gets your heart rate up without a single jump.

Stand tall — don't hunch forward as you speed up.

30 sec
—
04

Standing Calf Raise

Rise onto your toes, pause, lower slow. Easy to underestimate until your calves are burning by rep 15.

Hold a wall or chair for balance if needed.

20 reps
—
05

Standing Side Bend

Reach one arm overhead and lean gently to the opposite side. A controlled stretch-and-strengthen move that loosens up a day of sitting in transit.

Move slowly — this isn't a bounce, it's a reach.

8 / side
Rest 45 sec, repeat circuit

Make it harder or easier

Same circuit, scaled to your energy level after a travel day.

Bodyweight Squat, scaled

Easier — Squat to touch the edge of the bed, then stand. Reduces range of motion.
Standard — 15 reps, full depth, steady pace.
Harder — Add a 2-second pause at the bottom of every rep.
