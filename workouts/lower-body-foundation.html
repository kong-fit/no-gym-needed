Lower Body
Foundation

Squat and lunge patterns that build real leg strength using nothing but your bodyweight and gravity. This is the closest thing on the site to an actual leg day.

7
Exercises
3
Rounds

The circuit

Complete all 7 moves, rest 90 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds.

01

Bodyweight Squat

The foundation move of the whole session. Full depth, controlled tempo — this is where most of the work happens.

Knees track over your toes, not caving inward.

15 reps
—
02

Walking Lunge

Step forward into a lunge, then bring your back foot through to step into the next one. Builds single-leg strength and balance together.

Front knee stays stacked over your ankle at the bottom.

10 / side
—
03

Single-Leg Glute Bridge

One foot on the ground, the other extended straight, hips lifting and lowering. Isolates each glute far harder than a two-leg bridge.

Keep hips square — don't let one side rotate open.

10 / side
—
04

Sumo Squat

Feet wide, toes turned out, squat straight down. Shifts the emphasis to your inner thighs and glutes compared to a standard squat.

Push your knees out in the same direction as your toes.

15 reps
—
05

Reverse Lunge

Step back instead of forward — easier on the knees than a forward lunge while still building single-leg strength.

Lower straight down, not forward over your toes.

10 / side
—
06

Calf Raise

Slow and controlled, full range from heels down to toes up. The often-skipped muscle that makes the rest of your legs look finished.

Pause for a full second at the top of every rep.

20 reps
—
07

Wall Sit

Finishes every round with an isometric burnout — by round 3 this will feel a lot longer than 30 seconds.

Breathe steadily — holding your breath makes it harder, not easier.

30 sec hold
Rest 90 sec, repeat circuit

Make it harder or easier

Same circuit, scaled to your current strength level.

Walking Lunge, scaled

Easier — Reverse lunges in place instead of walking, holding a wall for balance.
Standard — 10 walking lunges per side, full room length.
Harder — Add a 2-second pause at the bottom of each step.
