Cardio Without
Cardio Gear

Interval-based conditioning that gets your heart rate up without a treadmill, bike, or rower in sight. This one has impact — if you're on a floor with neighbors below, try the Apartment Burner instead.

The circuit

40 seconds of work, 20 seconds of rest, for each move. Complete all 5, rest 90 seconds, repeat for 4 rounds.

01

Jumping Jacks

The simplest way to spike your heart rate fast. Keep a steady, sustainable pace rather than going all-out immediately.

Land soft on the balls of your feet, not flat-footed.

40 sec
Rest 20 sec
02

High Knees

Quick, alternating knee drives toward your chest, almost like running in place. By round 2 your legs will know exactly what this is.

Pump your arms in rhythm — it helps you keep pace.

40 sec
Rest 20 sec
03

Squat to Reach

A bodyweight squat that finishes with both arms reaching overhead as you stand. Adds an upper-body component to a lower-body move.

Reach with control — don't use momentum to throw your arms up.

40 sec
Rest 20 sec
04

Mountain Climbers

From a plank, drive your knees alternately toward your chest at speed. Trains your core and conditioning at the same time.

Keep your hips low — don't let them pike up as you fatigue.

40 sec
Rest 20 sec
05

Skater Hops

Lateral bounds from one foot to the other, like a speed skater. The most demanding move on this list — and the most effective.

Land soft and controlled — this is about balance, not just speed.

40 sec
Rest 90 sec, repeat circuit

Make it harder or easier

Same circuit, scaled to protect your joints and your downstairs neighbors.

Skater Hops, scaled

Easier — Step side to side instead of hopping, keeping both feet low to the ground.
Standard — Full lateral hop, landing soft on one foot before pushing to the other side.
Harder — Add a small pause and balance check on each landing before the next hop.
