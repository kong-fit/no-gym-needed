Morning
Mobility

A slow, deliberate sequence to undo a night of sleep and a day of sitting. No reps to count, no rounds to track — just move through each one with full attention.

The sequence

Move through these once, slowly. This isn't meant to be hard — it's meant to be thorough.

01

Cat-Cow

On hands and knees, alternate arching your back up and dropping it down. The classic spine-waker, and for good reason.

Move with your breath — round on the exhale, arch on the inhale.

8 reps
—
02

World's Greatest Stretch

From a lunge, drop your back knee down, rotate your torso toward your front leg, reaching one arm to the sky. Opens hips, spine, and shoulders in one move.

Keep your front foot flat the entire time.

5 / side
—
03

Standing Forward Fold

Hinge at your hips and let your upper body hang. Don't force it — gravity does most of the work here.

Bend your knees as much as you need to relax your back.

30 sec hold
—
04

Arm Circles

Big, slow circles forward then backward. Looks simple, does real work loosening up tight shoulders after a night of sleeping on them.

Make the circles as large as feels comfortable — don't rush them.

10 each way
—
05

Hip Circles

Hands on hips, draw slow circles with your hips in both directions. Loosens the joint that sitting tightens the most.

Keep your knees soft, not locked straight.

8 each way
—
06

Standing Quad Stretch

Pull one heel toward your glutes, holding for balance against a wall if needed. A direct counter to a day spent in a chair.

Keep your knees close together — don't let the lifted knee drift out.

20 sec / side
—
07

Deep Breathing (standing or seated)

Five slow breaths, in through the nose, out through the mouth. Closes the sequence by shifting your nervous system out of "just woke up" mode.

Let your exhale take longer than your inhale.

5 breaths
Done

Make it harder or easier

This sequence scales by depth, not difficulty.

World's Greatest Stretch, scaled

Easier — Skip the rotation, just hold the lunge with both hands on the floor.
Standard — Full rotation, 5 reps per side, reaching arm to the sky.
Harder — Hold the rotated position for 3 full breaths each side instead of moving through it.
