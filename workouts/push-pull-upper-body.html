Push-Pull
Upper Body

Push-ups, planks, and pulling substitutes that train your whole upper body without a single dumbbell. The honest truth: pulling is the hardest muscle group to train with no equipment — this is the best version possible.

6
Exercises
3
Rounds
60
Sec rest / round
15
Minutes

The circuit

Complete all 6 moves, rest 60 seconds, repeat for 3 rounds.

01

Standard Push-Up

The core pushing movement of the session. Full range, chest close to the floor at the bottom.

Keep your body in one straight line — no sagging hips.

10 reps
—
02

Towel Row (under a sturdy table)

Lie under a sturdy table, grip the edge, and pull your chest toward it. The closest equipment-free substitute for a real pulling exercise.

Only use a table that won't tip — test it with body weight first.

10 reps
—
03

Pike Push-Up

Hips lifted high, hands and feet on the floor, lower your head toward your hands. Shifts the work onto your shoulders.

Keep your legs as straight as your hamstrings allow.

8 reps
—
04

Prone Y-T-W Raise

Lying face down, lift your arms into a Y, then a T, then a W shape. Trains your upper back and rear shoulders — the muscles push-ups skip entirely.

Lift from your shoulder blades, not just your arms.

8 / shape
—
05

Diamond Push-Up

Hands close together under your chest, thumbs and index fingers touching. Shifts more load onto your triceps than a standard push-up.

Drop to knees if your elbows flare or your form breaks down.

8 reps
—
06

Side Plank (each side)

Closes the session with anti-rotation core work, which supports every pushing and pulling movement above it.

Stack your hips — don't let the top hip roll forward.

20 sec / side
Rest 60 sec, repeat circuit

Make it harder or easier

Same circuit, scaled to your current pushing strength.

Standard Push-Up, scaled

Easier — Knees down, or hands elevated on a sturdy step or couch edge.
Standard — Full push-up, 10 reps, controlled tempo.
Harder — Feet elevated on a chair, or add a 2-second pause at the bottom.
